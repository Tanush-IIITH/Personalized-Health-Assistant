# Demo Video Script

Duration target: 3 to 5 minutes
Recommended length: about 4 minutes

Audience:
- instructors
- evaluators
- project reviewers

Goal:
Show a believable end-to-end health journey across the Patient Mobile App and Doctor Web Dashboard:

1. patient uploads an older report
2. patient syncs wearable data
3. backend generates an alert and summary
4. doctor reviews the patient story
5. patient asks the voice assistant why the alert appeared

---

## Version To Read Aloud

### 0:00 - 0:20

"In this demo, we’re showing a complete patient-to-doctor health workflow using our system. We’ll follow one patient, Nisha Rao, who uploads an older lipid report, syncs wearable data, triggers a medium-risk alert, and then reviews the explanation through the app. We’ll also show how the doctor sees the same case from the dashboard."

### 0:20 - 0:50

"We start on the mobile app as Nisha. She uploads an older blood test, in this case a lipid panel from January. This is important because the app is not only reacting to live wearable data, it is also grounding decisions in historical clinical data. Once the report is uploaded, the backend processes it through OCR and structured extraction, so the cholesterol results become usable as health evidence."

### 0:50 - 1:20

"Next, Nisha connects and syncs wearable data. Over the last two days, the data shows a pattern that is mildly concerning: her resting heart rate is trending up, her activity is lower than usual, and her sleep is shorter. On their own, these signals may not be enough to justify escalation, but combined with the earlier cholesterol abnormality, they tell a much stronger story."

### 1:20 - 1:50

"Now we move to the system side effects. The nightly evaluation flow checks recent wearable signals together with structured report history. In this case, it identifies a meaningful cardiovascular-risk pattern and generates a medium-severity alert. The platform also creates a summary so that both the patient and the doctor can immediately understand what changed and why it matters."

### 1:50 - 2:35

"Now we switch to the doctor dashboard. Dr. Arjun Mehta logs in and sees Nisha in the patient list. From the doctor’s view, the case is easy to understand quickly: there is a medium alert, there is historical lipid evidence behind it, and there is recent wearable drift that supports the change in status. The doctor can open the patient summary, view the report context, and understand that this is not an isolated event but a pattern emerging across multiple inputs."

### 2:35 - 3:15

"We then return to the patient experience. On the mobile app, Nisha sees that an alert has appeared, and instead of reading through raw data, she can ask the assistant a natural question: 'Why did I get an alert?' The assistant answers in grounded language, explaining that the system combined her older cholesterol report with recent wearable trends like elevated resting heart rate, lower activity, and shorter sleep."

### 3:15 - 3:45

"This is the key value of the system: it connects historical reports, continuous wearable monitoring, alert generation, doctor review, and patient-facing explanation in one coherent flow. The patient gets clarity, the doctor gets context, and both sides see the same evidence-backed story."

### 3:45 - 4:00

"That completes the demo. We’ve shown how one realistic health scenario moves smoothly from ingestion, to monitoring, to alerting, to clinical review, and finally to explainable patient interaction."

## Shorter Backup Script

Use this if you need a tighter 3-minute cut.

"This demo follows a complete health journey across our patient app and doctor dashboard. First, the patient uploads an older lipid report. The backend extracts structured lab data so previous cholesterol abnormalities become usable evidence. Next, the patient syncs wearable data, and the latest readings show elevated resting heart rate, lower activity, and shorter sleep. The nightly rules flow combines those recent wearable signals with the older report and generates a medium-severity alert. On the doctor dashboard, the clinician can immediately see the patient, the alert, and the summary that explains the trend. Finally, the patient opens the voice assistant and asks, 'Why did I get an alert?' The response is grounded in both report history and wearable changes. This shows the full value of the system: historical reports, live monitoring, alerts, summaries, doctor visibility, and explainable patient guidance all working together in one connected workflow."
